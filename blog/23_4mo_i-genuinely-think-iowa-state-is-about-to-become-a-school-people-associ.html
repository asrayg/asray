Why I Am Bullish on Iowa State Quant

4mo • Edited · source

Original post opener: I genuinely think Iowa State is about to become a school people associate with quant and high finance. I am bullish on this opinion.

I genuinely think Iowa State is about to become a school people associate with quant and high finance. I am bullish on this opinion.

For a long time, if you wanted to break into Wall Street from the Midwest, it felt like you had to overcompensate. Now I’m watching a shift happen in real time.

The ISU Algorithmic Trading Club is building real infrastructure. We’re not just talking about markets; we’re studying microstructure, execution, prediction markets, derivatives, and actually writing the systems behind it. It feels less like a “club” and more like a lab for market builders.

At the same time, The Ivy Investment Banking Initiative is sharpening the traditional Wall Street path. Technical prep is serious. Networking is intentional. People are treating recruiting like a craft.

And then you have the Iowa State Investment Group managing a six-figure portfolio. Real capital. Real accountability. Students learning Bloomberg, doing equity research, thinking about allocation decisions that actually matter.

There’s something different in the air. You’ll overhear conversations about volatility, order flow, and macro positioning between classes. You’ll see students shipping backtests at 2 am and then prepping for technicals the next morning.

Wall Street and recruiters should probably start paying attention to Iowa State.

We’re not supposed to be a quant pipeline school.

That’s exactly why we might become one.
