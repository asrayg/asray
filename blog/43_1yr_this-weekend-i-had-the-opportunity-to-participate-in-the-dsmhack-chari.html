This weekend, I had the opportunity to participate in the dsmHack Charity Hackathon with Eli Newland, Andreas Christianson, Owenn Herrmann, Arjun Patel, and Justin Moran.

We worked with a Des Moines based non-profit called Neighborhood Finance Corporation (NFC) to help rebuild their map system, making it more efficient and eliminating the need for expensive software development consulting fees. Check out what we built at nfc.how

I really enjoyed this version of hackathon because it focused on helping charities with their technology needs. One key consideration was ensuring that maintenance costs were kept as low as possible while also making the code easy to manage. We used GitHub Actions and Pages to streamline code maintenance and wrote extensive documentation to enable them to maintain it on their own.

I encourage anyone in the Des Moines region working in software development to attend dsmHack next year. Charities and nonprofits will always need technology assistance, and this is a fantastic opportunity to apply your skills and make a real impact.
