I had the great opportunity to attend and represent Iowa State at the Okoboji Entrepreneurship Institute, a week-long entrepreneurship bootcamp and Iowa’s premier entrepreneurship event, during the second week of August. I learned valuable business and entrepreneurship insights from Iowa’s top business owners. The event was hosted in Okoboji, and the sense of community there is truly unmatched.

A recurring theme throughout the week was the importance of persistence, which is key to being a successful entrepreneur. This is something I apply in my work with The Brik, herdmate.app, and Scalar News.

I also had the chance to connect with other student entrepreneurs from colleges across Iowa, and it was inspiring to meet like-minded individuals. It pays to go above and beyond, and that’s especially evident in entrepreneurship.

While I was sad to leave Okoboji, I’m excited about the future and the goals I’m striving to achieve.

Huge thank you to the ISU Pappajohn Center for Entrepreneurship and Judi Eyles for setting this event up. Thank you to all the coaches and speakers, you guys helped me grow a lot during that week. Shout out to Dave Sly and Mellisa Sly household for taking me, Nathan Crooker, and Colin Speikers in for the week. And finally thanks for anyone who supported the Institute!
