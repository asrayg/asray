Honestly, something I’ve been noticing in conversations around Iowa State lately: entrepreneurship isn’t for everyone.

Innovation is.

Right now, there’s this weird cultural push where it feels like everyone is supposed to start a company. Raise money. Be a founder. Build a brand. Pitch decks, Delaware C-Corps, the whole thing.

Don’t get me wrong. I love entrepreneurship. A lot of my life revolves around it. But it’s not the only way to make cool things happen.

What we should actually be pushing is innovation.

A nurse who redesigns how patient intake works so people aren’t waiting forever? That’s innovation.

A teacher using AI tools to help struggling students understand material better? Innovation.

An engineer inside a big company who automates some annoying manual process and saves hundreds of hours? Innovation.

A finance analyst who builds a better model instead of recycling last year’s spreadsheet? Innovation.

A student who notices a broken campus system and just builds a quick tool to fix it? Innovation.

You don’t need a Delaware C-Corp to make something better.

Entrepreneurship is just one path.

Innovation is more of a mindset: curiosity, ownership, and the instinct to look at something and think “this could probably be better” and then actually doing something about it.

The title “founder” matters way less than that mindset.
